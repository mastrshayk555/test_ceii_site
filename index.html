ABOUT US

Community Enrichment Initiatives Inc. (CEII) is a non-profit corporation that enriches programs and enhances the facilities at the MLK Center. CEII builds relationships through thoughtful collaboration with community non-profits and local businesses. These combined efforts are the basis for the sustainability proposal for energy conservation, kitchen improvements and park enhancements for the MLK Center approved by the Wilmington City Council.

OUR MISSION

CEII mission is to is to enrich the lives of those in need in our community through initiatives that promote personal empowerment, self-sufficiency and productivity

The Organization

Community Enrichment Initiatives Inc. (CEII) is a 501(c)3 nonprofit corporation created in 2013 to enrich the lives of those in need in our community through initiatives that promote personal empowerment, self-sufficiency and productivity. To accomplish our mission we fight hunger and poverty through short and long term sustainable initiatives that promote health and wellness, personal empowerment, education and self-sufficiency in the Wilmington community.

We are governed by a volunteer Board of Directors who work as Project Managers for our various programs and projects. We leverage our resources and operationalize our service delivery through public/private collaborations with government, other non-profits, local businesses and teams of dedicated volunteers. We move forward with plans for programs and projects as resources and funds are available. Our current source of funds are donations and grants. A priority is to develop an income stream and generate revenue to pay ongoing operating expenses. In this way we create sustainable opportunities in our community and generate income to sustain our efforts.

In addition to regular programs that CEII supports at the MLK Center, CEII has two major projects underway the:

  1. MLK Community Kitchen
  2. Willowdale Farms

A Letter From Our Founder

I want to share a story about something that has been deeply meaningful to me. As a high school student, I volunteered at the Martin Luther King Community Center in downtown Wilmington, NC. Over time, I began to understand the pressing needs of the students, their parents, and the community itself. I realized that just a few additional resources could make a significant difference, but I was faced with many questions: Where would these resources come from? How could we use them most effectively?

I saw the potential in my students and the possibilities for the center. With that vision in mind, I decided to contribute $2,000 from my summer job savings and appealed to the community to match my donation in order to enhance the programs at the Center.

That effort led to the founding of the nonprofit Community Enrichment Initiatives, Inc(CEII). a year later. A dedicated board of volunteers came together with the mission of serving our community's needs and empowering individuals. The MLK Center, which was the initial inspiration for CEII, continues to be the heart of our efforts. Our mission remains focused on promoting personal empowerment, self-sufficiency, and productivity in our community.

Thank you for your support and for being part of this journey.

Warm regards,

Bonnie Hu

A Letter From Our Willowdale Farms Founder

After a full career as a Mechanical Engineer, I retired to Wilmington, NC to be closer the my Daughter's family and see my two grandchildren grow up and be a part of their lives.

One of my retirement bucket-list items was to become a Master Gardener. My mother had a garden and it brought joy and longevity into her life. I completed the Master Gardener training at NC Cooperative Extension in New Hanover County and spread the information to our community. I wanted to do something to give back to the community where my family lives; so, I started the non-profit association Wilmington Green to create and maintain sustainable community gardens. Initially, my mission was to create placemaking opportunities for the garden participants to enrich their lives but I soon learned there was a greater need.

Along the way, I was associated with Feast Down East when it organized the Cape Fear Food Council. One of our associate members was the Food Bank of Central and Eastern North Carolina. I learned just how bad food insecurity was in New Hanover County: there were over 62,000 people who were food insecure and one out of every five children in New Hanover County under eighteen was food insecure. Food insecurity and hunger were more urgent needs than placemaking so we revised our mission to support our food insecure neighbors.

One of our community gardens was installed at the Heart Center at the County Hospital. I learned our general nutrition was not good and the disastrous effect it had on our health- heart attacks, diabetes, cancer, hypertension and obesity. I expanded our mission to educate others about the benefits of eating healthier foods.

In 2018 I merged Wilmington Green into Community Enrichment Initiatives Inc. (CEII) to benefit from their 501c3 nonprofit tax exempt status and for long term sustainability of the community gardens. Wilmington Green became a project of CEII and is now known as Willowdale Farms.

We developed ten community gardens and the two acre Willowdale Farm. We have a full size commercial greenhouse to grow transplants for all of our community gardens. We have an outdoor kitchen to field wash and pack vegetables for distribution and to demonstrate healthy cooking and for tastings. We built a barn for more storage space.

We assisted dozens of local schools and gardens with technical support and free transplants. We thrive because of our dedicated volunteers and community collaborations.

My mom lived to be 94; and I think she would be proud of our work at Willowdale Farms. We work to “Build a Stronger Healthier Community”!


David C. Brenner

Certified Master Gardener

Founder of Wilmington Green

Willowdale Farms Manager

CEII - Board Member
