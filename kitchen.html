MLK Community Kitchen Project

A major CEII collaborative project at the MLK Center is the MLK Community Kitchen that will support creative solutions to health-related issues and improvement opportunities vital to our minority populations.

We will utilize this community development asset as a Teaching Kitchen to expand food education and wellness programs to encourage healthy living. As a Makers Space the Kitchen will serve as a foundation to develop and improve economic opportunity with food business education and culinary entrepreneurships and start-ups. The Kitchen will be a Processing Center for fresh produce from our community gardens to address food insecurity. Food insecurity is a debilitating issue in Wilmington's low to moderate income areas, particularly among minorities and the most vulnerable, the elderly and children living in poverty (27,253 people 2020 US Census). Our target population lives in and around the MLK Center in Wilmington and New Hanover County, and are primarily members of African-American and Hispanic minority group.
